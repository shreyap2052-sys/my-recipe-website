    2. Aloo Tikki Chaat

      3. Aloo Tikki Chaat is the ultimate "heartbeat" of Indian street food. In a nutshell, it is a savoury potato snack consisting of crispy, spiced patties (tikkis) served with a variety of toppings that hit every taste bud at once: sweet, spicy, tangy, and salty.

      Here's How to Make It :-

      1. Prep the Tikki: Mix boiled, mashed potatoes with cornflour (for binding), red chilli powder, and salt. Shape into patties and shallow-fry on a tawa until golden-brown and extra crisp.

      2. Prepare Chole: Use leftover chickpea curry or quickly sauté boiled chickpeas with chole masala, amchur (mango powder), and ginger. Keep it "saucy" rather than dry.

      3. The Base Assembly: Place two hot tikkis on a plate and slightly crush them to let the flavors soak in. Ladle a generous amount of hot chole over them.

      4. The Toppings: Pour fresh, whisked yogurt (dahi), spicy green mint chutney, and sweet-tangy tamarind chutney.

      5. Final Garnish: Sprinkle chaat masala, roasted cumin powder, and a handful of fine sev. Add chopped onions, coriander, and pomegranate seeds for a final pop of color.

    3. Sweet Delights

      4. <>

      Indulge in a trio of decadent "Sweet Delight" treats, starting with brownies, which offer a rich, dense chocolate experience that strikes a perfect balance between a chewy cookie and a soft cake, often featuring a glossy crust and optional add-ins like nuts or chocolate chips. For a more dramatic dessert, the Choco Lava cake—also known as a molten chocolate cake—consists of a delicate, warm cake exterior that, when cut, releases a luscious liquid chocolate center that flows like lava. Completing this indulgent selection are donut cakes, a delightful mashup featuring the moist, tender crumb of a classic cake in the iconic ring shape of a donut, typically finished with a sweet glaze, creamy frosting, or even a rich chocolate cream filling.


      Here's How to Make these :-

      1. Fudgy Small-Batch Brownies ~~

        • Melt: Combine 1/4 cup melted butter with 1/2 cup sugar and 1.5 tsp oil; whisk well.

        • Mix: Add 1 egg and 1 tsp vanilla; whisk until creamy.

        • Fold: Sift in 1/4 cup flour, 1/4 cup cocoa, and a pinch of salt. Gently fold until just combined (do not overmix).

        • Bake: Pour into a lined pan and bake at 175°C (350°F) for 17–19 minutes.

      2. 15-Minute Choco Lava Cake ~~

        • Melt: Melt 20g dark chocolate and 25g butter together; let it cool.

        • Whisk: In another bowl, whisk 1 egg and 15g sugar.

        • Combine: Pour the chocolate mixture into the eggs and stir in 10g flour.

        • Center: Pour into a greased ramekin and push a large chunk of chocolate into the center.

        • Bake: Bake at 180°C (350°F) for only 8 minutes. Eat immediately.

      3. Baked Donut Cakes ~~

        • Wet Mix: Whisk 1 egg, 1/2 cup milk, 1 tbsp melted butter, and 3/4 cup sugar.

        • Dry Mix: Sift in 1 cup flour, 1 tsp baking powder, and a pinch of salt and cinnamon.

        • Fold: Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet until a smooth batter forms.

        • Bake: Fill a greased donut pan and bake at 175°C (350°F) for 17 minutes.

        • Glaze: While warm, dip in melted butter, then roll in cinnamon sugar.
